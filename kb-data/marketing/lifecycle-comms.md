# Lifecycle Communications

## Quick Reference

- Lifecycle emails are a **profit lever**, not just a channel
- $91,608 incremental revenue from Black Friday campaign alone
- 51% open rate, 6.3% click rate across lifecycle emails
- Distinct from machine purchase communications — these support the coffee subscription journey
- Compounds with [[ftbp|FTBP]] acquisition engine to drive retention and LTV

## Lifecycle as Profit Lever

Lifecycle communications are positioned as a revenue driver, not a cost center. Evidence from the Black Friday campaign:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Incremental revenue | $91,608 AUD |
| Open rate | 51% |
| Click rate | 6.3% |
| Signal | Repeatable conversion signal |

These results demonstrate that well-timed, targeted lifecycle emails generate measurable incremental revenue, establishing a repeatable playbook for future campaigns.

## How Lifecycle Compounds the Growth Engine

[[ftbp|Fast-Track]] is beanz.com's primary acquisition engine:

- 193K sign-ups in 2 years
- 13% attachment to paid beanz conversions
- $353 average subscriber LTV

Lifecycle comms compound this engine by nurturing subscribers after acquisition, extending the revenue generated from each FTBP-acquired customer through ongoing engagement, cross-sell, and win-back communications.

## Lifecycle Email Types

Lifecycle emails support the lifetime of coffee ownership. They are distinct from machine purchase communications (handled by Breville).

| Stage | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| **Onboarding** | Welcome new subscribers, set expectations, drive first positive experience |
| **Engagement** | Regular touchpoints, coffee education, roaster stories |
| **Win-back** | Re-engage lapsed or at-risk subscribers |
| **Cross-sell** | Accessories, larger bags, seasonal and holiday SKUs |
