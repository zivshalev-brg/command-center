# Affordability Economics

## Quick Reference

- 1kg/2lb bags offer 15–20% savings over small bags across all four markets
- With Fast Track discount, specialty coffee costs less than supermarket brands
- Large bags account for 20% of total beanz volume in FY26H1
- Fast Track cohort over-indexing at 23% large bag mix, with 30% mix in December 2025
- Positioning: "Extra Virgin Olive Oil for the price of Canola Oil"

## Market Pricing

| Market | Small Bag | Large Bag (1kg/2lb) | Savings | With Fast Track |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|---------|----------------|
| AU | $19.95 | $63.95 | 19% | $48.00 |
| UK | £10.00 | £32.00 | 20% | £24.00 |
| DE | €12.90 | €43.90 | 15% | €33.00 |
| US | $19.95 | $44.95 | 15% | $33.70 |

The value proposition is clear: with the Fast Track discount, specialty coffee from independent roasters on beanz.com is priced below supermarket coffee brands.

## Volume Adoption

Large bags accounted for 20% of total beanz volume in FY26H1 (Jul–Dec 2025).

The [[ftbp|Fast Track]] cohort over-indexes at 23% large bag mix, driven by a strong uplift to 30% in December 2025. This indicates that new subscribers acquired through FTBP are more likely to choose the larger, more economical bag size.

FTBP volumes grew from 1,351 KG in July to 2,265 KG in December — a 68% increase within the half, accelerated by the FTBP v2 launch in October.

## Strategic Role

Affordability economics serves as a margin lever in the FY26 growth strategy under the "Deliver World-Class Customer Experience" pillar. Large bags as default:

- Increases average order value
- Reduces packaging and shipping cost per kilogram
- Improves customer value perception
- Drives retention through better price-per-cup economics

In FY27, 1kg affordability is a key lever for improving [[ftbp|FTBP conversion]] rates.
