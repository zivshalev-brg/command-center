## Executive Summary

**TL;DR:** Platform team identified significant complexity in the Operation Freedom bundle's gift card approach and pivoted discussion toward a simpler promo code or credit system for the MVP test.

**Overview:** Justin walked the team through [[operation-freedom|Operation Freedom]] — a Barista Express + Beanz gift card bundle offered via Klarna BNPL in the UK. The team surfaced critical questions about Klarna payment capture mechanics, GiveX financial risk (funds irrecoverable once loaded), soft bundle SKU logic deviations, and double-dip prevention with [[ftbp|FTBP]]. Discussion shifted from the original gift card approach toward a simpler promo code or credit-based system that avoids GiveX entirely, reduces fraud risk, and could serve as a foundation for a future loyalty scheme. The proposed May 15 launch date was confirmed as not feasible — no agreed date exists.

**Key Takeaways:**
- The GiveX gift card approach carries significant financial risk: funds paid to GiveX are irrecoverable if the customer doesn't redeem, and Breville loses both the transaction and the gift card value in return/cancellation scenarios
- Existing soft bundle SKU logic will not work as-is — the Operation Freedom pricing model (50% off machine display + separate gift card pricing) deviates from the proportional discount calculation used today
- A promo code or credit-based system is emerging as the preferred MVP approach: simpler to implement, avoids GiveX risk, and could be managed manually for a 1-month proof of concept
- Klarna payment integration needs investigation — this is a different structure to today's Klarna setup (zero-interest, different merchant fee) and may require a separate agreement or pipeline through Adyen
- Gift cards work cross-brand (Sage/Breville/Beanz within a region), which raises questions about restricting the high-value bundle gift card from appearing on other brand storefronts
